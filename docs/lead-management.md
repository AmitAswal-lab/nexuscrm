# Lead and client management

## Product model

Nexus CRM stores leads and clients in one stable contact record. Converting a
lead changes its kind from `lead` to `client`; it does not copy or replace the
document. Future tasks, calls, and activity can therefore keep the same
`contactId`.

Version 1 treats a contact as one person with an optional company. At least one
of email or phone is required.

## Firestore schema

Contacts are stored at:

```text
workspaces/{workspaceId}/contacts/{contactId}
```

Important fields:

```text
workspaceId
kind                    lead | client
fullName
companyName             nullable
email                   nullable
phone                   nullable
notes                   nullable
ownerId                 nullable sales-representative UID
leadStage               new | contacted | qualified | proposal | lost | null
isArchived
createdByUserId
updatedByUserId
createdAt
updatedAt
convertedAt             nullable
convertedByUserId       nullable
```

Timestamps are generated by Firestore. Client records require conversion
metadata and cannot retain a lead stage.

Administrator assignment uses active membership documents at:

```text
workspaces/{workspaceId}/members/{userId}
```

Active sales membership documents must include `displayName` and `email` for
the assignee selector.

## Permissions

- An active administrator can read and manage all contacts in the workspace.
- An active sales representative can query, read, and update only contacts
  whose `ownerId` matches their Firebase UID.
- Sales-created leads are always self-assigned.
- Administrators may assign an active sales representative or leave a contact
  unassigned.
- Membership and profile writes remain backend-trusted.
- Hard deletion is denied. Archive is a validated soft update.

Router guards provide role-appropriate navigation, while Firestore rules are
the authoritative security boundary.

## Contact lifecycle

1. A lead is created with a basic stage.
2. Common contact information, notes, ownership, and stage can be edited.
3. Conversion atomically changes a lead into a client and records the actor and
   server timestamp.
4. Archive removes the contact from active queries without deleting its record.

Conversion and archive operations use Firestore transactions to validate the
current document type and prevent stale or conflicting writes.

The shared list and forms receive role-specific access and ownership rules from
their route builders.

## State management

- `ContactListCubit` owns the role-scoped contact stream and local type filter.
- `LeadFormCubit` owns creation, validation-adjacent submission state, and the
  administrator assignee stream.
- `ContactDetailCubit` owns one live contact subscription.
- `ContactEditCubit` resolves contact and assignee data before updates.
- `ContactActionsCubit` isolates conversion and archive mutations.
- Contact details resolve active sales-owner names for administrators and link
  to call-note and activity-timeline workflows.
- `SalesDashboardCubit` derives owner-scoped metrics and recent contacts.

Domain contracts remain independent of Flutter and Firebase. Firestore mapping
and typed failure translation stay in the data layer.

## Queries and indexes

Active workspace queries filter `isArchived` and order by `updatedAt`.
Sales queries additionally filter `ownerId`. Composite indexes for those query
shapes are defined in `firestore.indexes.json`.

Reviewed rules and indexes are deployed to the development Firebase project:
`nexuscrm-dev-amitaswal`.

## Deferred work

Tasks, follow-ups, calls, and activity history will reference the stable
`contactId` in later milestones. Restore, hard delete, bulk import, company
entities, and cross-workspace contact movement are outside the current MVP
scope.
